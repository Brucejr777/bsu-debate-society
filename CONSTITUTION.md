# CONSTITUTION

**Article 1.
THE ORGANIZATION**
The name of the organization shall be The Debate Society. Herein, it shall be referred to as “The
Society.”
**Article 2.
VISION AND MISSION**
Section 1. _Mission._ – The Society envisions a vibrant, inclusive, and intellectually stimulating
community where individuals from diverse backgrounds come together to engage in respectful
discourse.
Section 2. _Vision._ – The Society is dedicated to fostering critical thinking, effective
communication, and a spirit of intellectual curiosity.
**Article 3.
DECLARATION OF PRINCIPLES**
Section 1. The Society shall uphold the transformative power of reasoned dialogue. It recognizes
that civil and intellectual debate is not merely an exercise in rhetoric, but a lifelong discipline
essential to the pursuit of truth, critical thought, and personal development.
Section 2. The Society shall promote and protect the principles of diversity and inclusion. It
affirms the importance of integrating the contributions, presence, and perspectives of individuals
from various backgrounds into the community, and shall actively advocate for awareness of these
values among its members and the broader local community.
Section 3. The Society shall adhere to the principles of democratic leadership, discipline,
cooperation, industry, brotherhood, and harmony among its constituents, and shall provide
appropriate avenues for the development and application of such competencies.


Section 4. The Society shall recognize and uphold the vital ties among students, faculty,
administration, and other sectors of the academic community within Benguet State University. It
shall serve as a platform for the peaceful and intellectual discussion of issues, concerns, and
institutional initiatives.
Section 5. The Society and its members shall uphold the highest standards of honesty, fairness,
and ethical conduct. All actions undertaken in the name of the Society shall be guided by a
shared sense of responsibility: to truth, to one another, and to the duties entrusted to each
member.
Section 6. The Society shall promote an enduring appreciation of Filipino culture, heritage, and
values. It shall advocate for a form of patriotism grounded in critical love, compassion, civic
engagement, and social responsibility, and shall reject all expressions of exclusion, hostility, or
prejudice.
Section 7. The Society shall cultivate a spirit of constructive and healthy competition among its
members and constituent Houses. All competitions and contests shall be designed to inspire
excellence, deepen commitment, and strengthen both individual character and collective bonds.
Section 8. The Society shall preserve its meaningful traditions while remaining open to
innovation and reform. It affirms that institutional relevance requires adaptability, and that
traditions must evolve in response to the changing needs of its members and the broader society.
Section 9. The Society shall regard membership not merely as a temporary affiliation, but as a
continuous process of growth: intellectually, socially, and morally. It shall strive to form
individuals who are articulate, principled, and prepared to lead both within and beyond the
organization.
Section 10. The Society shall affirm that education is not solely the acquisition of knowledge,
but also the cultivation of awareness, empathy, and agency. It shall serve as a platform for
empowerment through discourse, recognizing that learning is a means to liberation, personally
socially, and morally.


**Article 4.
RIGHTS OF THE MEMBERS**
Section 1. The right of all the members for freedom to express, freedom of speech, and freedom
to peaceful assembly, not contrary to the Constitution and Policies of the Society and existing
laws governing such rights, against the Society.
Section 2. The right of all members to file a candidacy or be appointed for the highest governing
body of the Society or their respective House’s governing body position as prescribed by the
provisions of this Constitution.
Section 3. The right to exercise suffrage, ensuring their participation in choosing representatives
and voicing opinions on Society's governance matters.
Section 4. The right of all members to petition the proper authorities through the organization for
redress of grievances, and present views and opinions conducive to broad, sound, and healthy
thinking of vital issues concerning the welfare of the students.
Section 5. The right of all members to information on matters of public concern shall be
recognized. Access to official records, documents, and papers pertaining to official acts,
transactions, or decisions, as well as to research data used as basis for policy development, shall
be afforded by all members, subject to such limitations as may be provided by the Constitution
and Policies of the Society.
Section 6. The right of every member to be informed of the rules and regulations governing him.
Section 7. No member shall be subjected to exploitation, involuntary servitude or cruel or
unusual punishment by the highest governing body of the Society or their respective House’s
highest governing body.
Section 8. The right of all members to form, assist, and join school-based and non-school-based
organizations, alliances, and federations for their integral development.


Section 9. The rights of all members to avail all the facilities and resources of the organization
for school related activities within the school campus, subject to limitations as may be provided
by their respective House’s highest governing body or the highest governing body of the Society.
Section 10. No member shall be subject to punishments, sanctions, or fines, solely by reason of
his political beliefs and aspirations.
Section 11. In all punishment prosecutions of the highest governing body of the Society, the
accused shall be presumed innocent until the contrary is proved, and shall enjoy the right to be
heard by himself and counsel, to be informed of the nature and cause of the accusation against
him, to have a speedy, impartial, and public trial, to meet the witnesses face to face, and to have
compulsory process to secure the attendance of witnesses and the production of evidence in his
behalf. However, after arraignment, the trial may proceed notwithstanding the absence of the
accused, provided that he has been duly notified and his failure to appear is unjustifiable.
Section 12. No member shall be compelled to be a witness against himself.
**Article 5.
MEMBERSHIP**
(1) The membership for the society shall be open to all students of Benguet State University,
regardless of their college, degree program, gender, class, race, political, religious, and/or other
personal affiliations.
(2) Admission into a House shall constitute Provisional Membership in the Society. To attain
Full Membership status, all provisional members must complete a mandatory one (1) academic
year of service within an Office of the High Council as an Executive Intern.
a. Every admitted member of a House is required to be deployed to one of the Executive Offices
of the High Council to assist the respective Secretaries in Society-wide operations.
b. This service year serves as the mandatory training ground for Society-wide standards,
ensuring every member understands and embodies the pillars of Manama, Bathala, Kabunian,
and Laon before being conferred permanent status.
c. Upon the successful completion of the one-year service and a satisfactory performance rating
from the supervising High Council Secretary, the individual shall be elevated to Full Member
status.


d. Executive Interns shall have the right to participate in House activities and debates; however,
they shall be restricted from voting on High Council-level Constitutional amendments and from
seeking elective office until their Full Membership is formally conferred.
(3) Each House shall have the exclusive right to recruit, evaluate, and accept its own members in
accordance with its internal membership policy, values, and traditions. Any eligible individual
who seeks to become part of the Society may do so by applying directly to one of the four
Houses. Admission into a House shall constitute formal membership in the Society. However, all
House recruitment must adhere to the following rules:
(a) All membership applications shall be approved by a majority vote of the respective House
Council.
(b) No individual may be a member of more than one House at a time.
(4) Each House shall maintain and publicly disclose its own Membership Policy, which must be
aligned with the Society’s Constitution and Policies, uphold the principles of fairness,
transparency, and merit, and be reviewed and approved by the President, with the concurrence of
the House Council, before implementation.
(5) Transfers between Houses may only occur during official transfer periods and require the
mutual consent of both Houses involved.
(6) The membership shall be valid for one [1] academic year and shall be renewed for the
following academic year. Renewal of membership to a House only requires the approval of the
House Council and does not need the approval of the President.
(7) Members shall abide by their respective House Charter, House policies, the Society
Constitution, and the Society policies.
(7) The High Council and the House Councils may sanction members for misconduct, based on
collected evidence, inclusion policy, and prior warnings.
(9) Every member of the Society is required to pay a membership fee at the commencement of
each semester. The specific amount of the fee shall be determined through a deliberation and
majority vote of the Council of House Chancellors, subject to the final approval of the President.


**Article 6.
THE SOCIETY HOUSES**
Section 1. Declaration of the Society Houses
The Society shall be composed of four permanent and chartered Houses, each representing a
unique philosophical, cultural, and symbolic identity that reflects the values of the Society. These
Houses shall serve as the foundational sub-organizations of the Society and shall act as the
primary units for competitive debate, truth-seeking, scholarly inquiry, community-building, and
leadership development.
Section 2. Establishment.
(1) There shall be four permanent Houses of the Society, established in honor of the four
foundational student leaders who chartered the Debate Society. Each House shall also maintain
its own motto, emblem, color, and values, subject to approval by the House Society. Houses shall
have autonomy over their membership policies, internal structure, traditions, and activities, so
long as they remain consistent with the Constitution and Policies of the Society. These Houses
shall be known as: House of Bathala, House of Laon, House of Manama, House of Kabunian.
(2) A group of Society members may apply for the formal establishment of a new House by
submitting a House Charter to the Council. The Charter must include, but shall not be limited to:
(a) The proposed House motto, emblem, and core values;
(b) The intended structure of its House Council;
(c) Its initial membership policy;
(d) A code of conduct and internal rules.
(3) All House Charters must be in full accordance with the Constitution and policies of the
Society. Any provisions inconsistent with Society principles shall be subject to revision or
rejection.
**Article 7.
THE HOUSE COUNCILS**
Section 1. Declaration of the House Councils.


(1) Each House shall be governed by a House Council, which shall serve as the highest
governing body within the House. It shall have full authority over the internal affairs of the
House, subject to the overarching Constitution and policies of the Society.
(2) All official House policies, amendments, and internal rules shall be enacted through formal
House Council Resolutions. A resolution shall require the concurrence of a majority of the sitting
House Council members and the approval of the House Chancellor to be considered valid. Each
resolution shall be properly documented, dated, and entered into the House Chronicle or
Records.
Section 2. The functions of the House Council shall be:
(a) Formulate and implement internal policies, plans, and initiatives consistent with the House
Charter, serve as the supreme decision-making authority within the House, and uphold and
promote the House's values, traditions, and identity;
(b) Manage the recruitment, selection, and induction of new members in accordance with the
approved House Membership Policy, monitor member participation, engagement, and standing
within the House, recommend or implement probationary systems, mentorship programs, and
similar member development efforts;
(c) Organize and coordinate worthwhile student activities designed to improve the general
welfare of the students, as well as to prepare them for constructive citizenship and to develop
sound and critical thinking among members;
(d) Represent the House in the Council of House Chancellors and in communications with the
Society High Council, ensure the House is informed of Society-wide policies, deadlines, and
events, and serve as the intermediary in matters requiring coordination between Houses;
(e) Enforce the House’s internal code of conduct and values, investigate and address minor
violations or conflicts among House members, and recommend disciplinary action, including
suspension or expulsion, subject to review by the High Council, through the Internal Affairs
Office, for serious offenses;
(f) Manage the House’s treasury, prepare and maintain transparent financial records and submit
them to the High Council, through the Finance and Resource Management Office, and ensure all
expenditures serve the interests and mission of the House;
(g) Maintain the integrity of the House’s motto, emblem, color, and cultural identity, document
important events, programs, and achievements in an official House chronicle or report, and
promote a sense of pride, continuity, and historical awareness among members;


(h) Regularly assess House operations and member satisfaction, submit semester reports on
House performance, activities, and membership to the Society Secretariat Director and propose
amendments to the House Charter, subject to the approval of the House members, with the
concurrence of the President.
(i) Execute specific mandates and duties as may be directed by the President, provided that such
instructions are conducive to the general welfare and holistic development of the Houses, the
Society, and the community at large.
Section 2. The House Council shall be the primary governing body of the House, composed of
officers duly elected or appointed to lead its various departments. The Council shall be organized
into Executive Offices, each overseen by a Director. The composition of the House Council shall
be determined through a formal House Council Resolution, approved by a majority of the
Council and signed by the House Chancellor. While each House may establish additional or
modified positions in accordance with its internal needs and traditions, the standard
organizational structure shall consist of the following:
(1) The Office of the House Chancellor.
The House Chancellor, who shall be elected by a direct vote from the House members, shall
serve as the head of the House Council.
The powers and duties of the House Chancellor shall be:
a. Presides over all House Council meetings and House Assemblies;
b. Approves House Council Resolutions;
c. Exercises executive oversight over House programs and governance;
d. Represents the House in official Society-wide assemblies and external affairs;
e. May veto any House Council Resolution, subject to override by a two-thirds vote; and
f. Shall be a member of the Council of House Chancellors.
g. Performs such other duties as may be assigned by the House Council or the President.
(2) The Office of the House Vice Chancellor.
The House Vice Chancellor shall serve as the second-in-rank of the House Council and shall be
elected by a direct vote of the House membership. The powers and duties of the House Vice
Chancellor shall be:


a. Assists and substitutes for the Chancellor in their absence;
b. Oversees coordination between officers and monitors internal execution;
c. Assists the House Chancellor in the preparation of reports for the Council of House
Chancellors;
d. Performs such other duties as may be assigned by the House Chancellor or the House Council.
(3) The Office of the Secretariat.
The Secretariat Director shall be appointed by the House Chancellor. The Director shall lead the
Office of the Secretariat and serve as the third-in-rank officer of the House Council. This
OfficeThe powers and duties of the Secretariat Office shall be:
a. The primary custodian of all House records, responsible for the drafting of official documents,
the recording of minutes for all House meetings and assemblies;
b. Shall maintain a systematic record of all House Council Resolutions and Policy Mandates,
ensuring they are accessible to all members for reference;
c. Shall be responsible for the documentation of the House Chronicle, recording the
achievements, major events, and historical milestones of the House during their term; and
d. Performs such other duties as may be assigned by the House Chancellor or the House Council.
(3) The Office of Internal Affairs
The Director of Internal Affairs shall be appointed by the respective House Chancellor. The
Director shall lead the Office of Internal Affairs and serve as the primary officer responsible for
the internal cohesion, discipline, and welfare of the House members. The powers and duties of
the Internal Affairs Office shall be:
a. Enforce the House’s internal code of conduct and values, ensuring that all members uphold the
prestige and integrity of the Society;
b. Maintain the official House Membership Database, monitor the participation, engagement, and
semesterly standing of each member;
c. Act as the primary mediator in internal disputes or conflicts among House members, fostering
a culture of mutual respect and cooperation;


d. Monitor the general well-being of members and oversee the implementation of probationary
systems, mentorship programs, and similar member-development efforts;
e. Propose, design, and implement programs and activities aimed at the holistic development of
members;
f. Investigate minor violations of House policies and recommend formal disciplinary actions to
the House Council, subject to the review of the Society’s Internal Affairs Office;
g. Manage the recruitment and selection of new members in accordance with the approved
House Membership Policy.
h. Performs such other duties as may be assigned by the House Chancellor or the House Council.
(4) The Office of External Affairs
The Director of External Affairs shall be appointed by the respective House Chancellor. The
director shall lead the Office of External Affairs and serve as the primary liaison between the
House and all entities beyond its internal membership. The powers and duties of the Director of
External Affairs shall be:
a. Serve as the primary point of contact for coordination with other Houses, fostering a spirit of
friendly rivalry and collaborative growth within the Society;
b. Assist the House Chancellor in representing the House’s interests before the Council of House
Chancellors and the Society High Council;
c. Propose and lead initiatives, programs, and partnerships that extend the House’s impact to the
community at large, aligning with the Society’s mission of constructive citizenship;
d. Identify and establish partnerships with external organizations, sponsors, or academic
institutions that can provide resources, venues, or opportunities for the House;
e. Lead the planning and execution of joint activities, inter-House competitions, and
Society-wide events, ensuring the House’s active and prestigious participation;
f. Performs such other duties as may be assigned by the House Chancellor or the House Council.
(5) The Office of Financial and Resource Affairs
The Director of Financial and Resource Affairs shall be appointed by the House Chancellor and
shall lead the Office of Financial and Resource Affairs. This Office shall serve as the chief fiscal
department and the quartermaster of the House. The powers and duties of the Financial and
Resource Affairs Office shall be:


a. Act as the primary custodian of the House Treasury, ensuring the secure collection,
management, and documentation of all funds, including membership dues and revenues;
b. Maintain a comprehensive and updated Inventory of House Assets; oversee the procurement,
systematic storage, and maintenance of all physical equipment, supplies, and digital resources;
c. Develop the semesterly House Budget in coordination with the House Chancellor, ensuring the
optimal allocation of resources toward the House’s strategic goals;
d. Manage the distribution and retrieval of House properties for official events, ensuring all
materials are functional, accounted for, and returned to storage;
e. Maintain a transparent and systematic ledger of all transactions; prepare and publish regular
financial and resource audits for review by the House Council;
f. Conduct periodic assessments of the condition of House equipment and propose the acquisition
or disposal of assets based on the House's operational needs;
g. Propose and implement programs focused on financial literacy, resource sustainability, and the
optimization of House assets for the benefit of the general membership;
h. Performs such other duties as may be assigned by the House Chancellor or the House Council.
(6) The Office of Business Affairs
The Director of Business Affairs shall be appointed by the House Chancellor and shall lead the
Office of Business Affairs. This Office shall serve as the primary revenue-generating and
entrepreneurial wing of the House. The powers and duties of the Business Affairs Office shall
be:
a. Conceptualize, propose, and spearhead Income-Generating Projects (IGPs) to ensure the
financial sustainability and independence of the House;
b. Manage the production and sale of official House merchandise, ensuring that all products
uphold the House’s visual identity and prestige;
c. Identify and negotiate partnerships, sponsorships, or advertisements with external entities and
local businesses to provide additional funding or resources for House projects;
d. Conduct surveys and research within the House and the wider Society to determine the
demand for specific products, services, or events;
e. Prepare comprehensive business plans, project proposals, and post-activity financial
statements for every IGP, ensuring all ventures are professional and transparent;


f. Propose and implement activities that develop the entrepreneurial skills of House members,
such as business pitching competitions or financial modeling workshops;
g. Perform such other duties as may be assigned by the House Chancellor or the House Council.
(7) The Office of Public Affairs
The Director of Public Affairs shall be appointed by the House Chancellor and shall lead the
Office of Public Affairs. This Office shall serve as the official media, branding, and information
wing of the House. The powers and duties of the Public Affairs Office shall be:
a. Serve as the primary manager of all official House social media accounts, ensuring the timely
posting of announcements, event updates, and member spotlights;
b. Lead the conceptualization and creation of all visual materials, including posters, infographics,
digital layouts, and promotional videos, ensuring they meet the House's aesthetic standards;
c. Act as the custodian of the House’s brand; ensure that all public-facing materials correctly use
the official House logos, colors, and typography;
d. Act as the public information office for the House, ensuring that all members and the general
public are well-informed of House activities, resolutions, and official stances;
e. Perform such other duties as may be assigned by the House Chancellor or the House Council.
**Article 8.
THE HIGH COUNCIL**
Section 1. Declaration of the High Council.
The High Council shall be the highest governing body of the BSU Debate Society. It shall have
jurisdiction over the Society Houses, including the House Councils, ensuring they adhere to the
Society’s policies.
Section 2. General Provisions of the High Council.
(1) The High Council shall be composed of offices elected or appointed in accordance with
the Constitution. The Council shall not be limited to the following offices: Office of the
President, Office of the Vice President, Office of the Executive Secretary, Office of
Internal Affairs, Office of External Affairs, Office of Business Affairs, Office of Finance


and Resource Management, Office of Public Affairs. Each offices shall be headed by a
Secretary.
(2) The President and Vice President shall be elected by a direct vote from the Society
members. The Executive Secretary and the Secretary of a respective office shall be
appointed by the President.
(3) All members of the High Council must be a bona fide member of the Society and have at
least one (1) semester of active participation in any House or committee of the Society.
The qualifications of the President shall include:
a. At the time of filing for candidacy, the individual must have served at least one
(1) full term in any of the following Principal Officer capacities: As a Secretary or
Vice President within the High Council; or As a House Chancellor, House Vice
Chancellor, Director within a House Council;
b. Must demonstrate a mastery of the Society’s Four Pillars: Manama, Bathala,
Laon, and Kabunian;
c. Must not have been found guilty of any major disciplinary violations within the
University or the Society;
d. Must have been a member of the Society for at least two (2) academic years,
which includes the mandatory year served as a Provisional Member;
e. Must not have served as the President of the Society.
The qualifications of the Vice President shall include:
f. At the time of filing for candidacy, the individual must have served at least one
(1) full term in any of the following Principal Officer capacities: As a Secretary
within the High Council; or As a House Chancellor, House Vice Chancellor,
Director within a House Council;
g. Must demonstrate a mastery of the Society’s Four Pillars: Manama, Bathala,
Laon, and Kabunian;
h. Must not have been found guilty of any major disciplinary violations within the
University or the Society;
i. Must have been a member of the Society for at least two (2) academic years,
which includes the mandatory year served as a Provisional Member.
The qualifications of the Executive Secretary shall include:
j. At the time of filing for candidacy, the individual must have served at least one
(1) full term in as a member of the High Council or their respective House
Council, not including their service as a Provisional Member;


k. Must demonstrate a mastery of the Society’s Four Pillars: Manama, Bathala,
Laon, and Kabunian;
l. Must not have been found guilty of any major disciplinary violations within the
University or the Society;
m. Must have been a member of the Society for at least two (2) academic years,
which includes the mandatory year served as a Provisional Member.
The qualifications of the Secretaries shall include:
n. Must have been a member of the Society for at least two (2) academic years
which includes the mandatory year served as a Provisional Member;
o. Must demonstrate a mastery of the Society’s Four Pillars: Manama, Bathala,
Laon, and Kabunian;
p. Must not have been found guilty of any major disciplinary violations within the
University or the Society;
(4) If an elected or appointed member of the High Council is terminated, asked to resign, or
impeached due to excessive absences or other grounds, then he or she shall not be able to
be appointed or elected to the High Council for the remainder of that academic year.
(5) The term of the Executive Secretary and the Secretary of the different Offices in the High
Council, with the exception of the appointed Vice President, after vacated by the elected
Vice President, shall be in concordance with the term of the President. In cases where the
President vacates his/her position due to impeachment, death, or ineligibility to perform
his/her duties, all appointed members shall also vacate their position, unless the next in
succession to be President wishes them to remain.
Section 4. The Office of the President
(1) The President shall serve as the Chief Executive of the Society and the ultimate presiding
authority of the High Council. The powers and duties of the President shall be:
a. Serve as the primary head of the Society and its official representative in all
external and university-level engagements;
b. Preside over all sessions of the High Council, with the authority to determine the
official agenda and order of business;
c. Serve as the Chairperson of the Supreme Council, exercising presiding authority
over all administrative trials, disciplinary hearings, and constitutional
interpretations of the Society;
d. Exercise the power to veto any society-wide resolution or policy passed by the
Council of House Chancellors. A presidential veto shall render the proposed


measure null and void unless challenged by the Council. The Council of House
Chancellors may override a presidential veto only through a unanimous vote of all
four (4) House Chancellors. Upon a unanimous override, the measure shall be
enacted immediately as Society policy, and the President shall have no further
power to block that specific resolution;
e. Spearhead the strategic execution of the Society’s long-term objectives and
mandates;
f. Ensure the rigorous enforcement of the Constitution, By-Laws, and High Council
Resolutions across all jurisdictions and Houses of the Society.
g. The President shall have the sole authority to negotiate and enter into formal
treaties, Memoranda of Agreement (MOA), and long-term alliances on behalf of
the Society. Such agreements shall require the ratification of the Council of House
Chancellors (COC) to be fully binding. All formal treaties, MOAs, or alliances
negotiated by individual Houses with external institutions must be approved by
the respective House Council and subsequently submitted to the President for
final review. No House-level agreement with an external entity shall be valid or
enforceable without the written approval and signature of the President. Such
House-level approvals do not require the ratification of the COC, provided they
do not affect other Houses or the Society as a whole;
h. The President shall appoint the Secretaries of the High Council and may create ad
hoc (temporary) committees or commissions to investigate specific issues or
execute special projects;
i. The President shall be mandated to deliver a formal State of the Society Address
to the Assembly of the Society (AS) one (1) week after the conclusion of each
semester. The President shall deliver a total of two (2) SOSAs throughout their
one-year term. The address shall serve as the official report on the Society’s
financial health, departmental progress and the vision of the President, and the
performance of the Four Houses;
j. In times of organizational crisis or extreme deadlock between Houses, the
President may issue Executive Orders to ensure the continuity of Society
operations. Such orders shall be temporary and remain in effect until the High
Council can convene to deliberate on a permanent solution;
k. The President shall exercise supreme oversight over the General Fund. While
House Treasuries remain autonomous, the President must ensure that
Society-wide funds are allocated in accordance with the pillars of the Society;
Section 5. The Office of the Vice President


(1) The Vice President shall be the second-highest executive officer of the Society and the
Chief Coordinator of its internal legislative affairs. The powers and duties of the Vice
President shall be:
a. Assume the full duties and presiding authority of the President in the event of
their absence, temporary incapacity, or at the President's express request;
b. Serve as the official presiding officer of the COC, responsible for convening
sessions, managing legislative deliberations, and ensuring the Houses act in
coordination;
c. The Vice President shall not have a regular vote within the Council of House
Chancellors except in the event of a deadlock (2-2 vote), in which case the Vice
President shall cast the deciding vote;
d. Monitor the internal affairs of the Four Houses to ensure their activities and
policies remain consistent with the Society’s Constitution and High Council
resolutions;
e. Spearhead the execution of Society-wide projects that require the joint
participation of all Four Houses.
Section 6. The Office of the Executive Secretary
(1) The Office of the Executive Secretary (OES) shall serve as the central administrative hub,
archive, and secretariat of the Society. It shall be composed of the Secretary as the chief
officer, the Assistant Secretary as the second-in-command of the office, Secretariat
Officer(s), Secretariat Intern(s) as the President may authorize.
(2) The powers and duties of the Office of the Executive Secretary shall include:
a. Monitor the implementation of mandates and directives issued by the President
across all Executive Offices, ensuring that all departmental actions are
documented and aligned with Society standards;
b. Act as the sole guardian of the original copy of the Constitution, the Great Seal of
the Society, and all historical archives, ensuring their absolute preservation and
security;
c. Draft and maintain the formal minutes of all sessions of the High Council and the
Supreme Council, providing the definitive and authenticated record of all Society
actions;
d. Maintain the Book of Policies (BOP) to track the status of all policies passed by
the Council of Chancellors, including those vetoed by the President or overridden
by the Council;
e. Exercise the authority to certify official documents, membership statuses, and
high-level communications. No act of the Society shall be considered official or
enforceable without the attestation of the Office;


f. Spearhead the orientation and pillar education for all incoming Provisional
Members, ensuring they are fully briefed on the Constitution and the Society’s
history before their deployment.
Section 7. The Office of Internal Affairs
(1) The Office of Internal Affairs (OIA) shall be the primary department responsible for the
welfare, ethical conduct, and internal cohesion of the Society. It shall be composed of the
Secretary of Internal Affairs as the Chief Officer, the Assistant Secretary as the
second-in-command, and such Internal Affairs Officers and Executive Interns as the
President may authorize.
(2) The powers and duties of the Office of Internal Affairs shall include:
a. Maintain the comprehensive and secure registry of all Society members. This
includes the authoritative tracking of an individual’s progression from Executive
Internship to Full Membership, alongside the verification of House affiliations
and residency requirements;
b. Design, propose, and spearhead the execution of Society-wide activities, events,
and programs specifically aimed at enhancing the holistic well-being and
inclusion of the membership. These programs may include mental health
initiatives, member-retention socials, and skill-building retreats;
c. Enforce the Society’s Rules and Procedure. The Office shall have the mandate to
investigate reports of misconduct, gather evidence, and recommend specific
disciplinary actions to the Supreme Council;
d. Coordinate with the Executive Secretary to assign Executive Interns to their
respective Executive Offices, ensuring a balanced distribution of manpower based
on the Intern’s skills and interests, and track their performance to ensure they
meet the criteria for elevation to Full Member status;
Section 8. The Office of External Affairs
(1) The Office of External Affairs (OEA) shall serve as the primary diplomatic and liaison
arm of the Society. It shall be composed of the Secretary of External Affairs as the Chief
Officer, the Assistant Secretary as the second-in-command, and such External Affairs

(2) The powers and duties of the Office of External Affairs shall include:

Section 9. The Office of Financial and Resource Affairs
(1) The Office of Financial and Resource Affairs (OFRA) shall be the primary department
responsible for the fiscal integrity, resource management, and logistical sustainability of
the Society. It shall be composed of the Secretary of Finance as the Chief Officer, the
Assistant Secretary as the second-in-command, and such Finance Officers and Executive
Interns as the President may authorize.
(2) The powers and duties of the Office of Financial and Resource Affairs shall include:
a. Exercise primary management over the General Fund, ensuring that all central
funds are collected, recorded, and deposited in accordance with the High
Council’s financial standards;
b. Coordinate with all Executive Offices to draft the Annual General Budget. The
Office shall review all funding requests before they are presented to the President,
after the concurrence of the Council of Chancellors, for signature;
c. Maintain a comprehensive inventory of all Society-owned physical and digital
assets. The Office shall be responsible for the procurement, maintenance, and
storage of Society equipment;
d. Issue a Monthly Fiscal Report to the High Council and a comprehensive Annual
Financial Statement to be included in the President’s State of the Society Address
(SOSA);
e. In coordination with the Office of Internal Affairs, the OFRA shall manage the
collection of membership dues, tournament fees, and sponsorship funds, ensuring
proper receipt and accounting.
Section 10. The Office of Business Affairs
(1) The Office of Business Affairs (OBA) shall serve as the primary commercial and
entrepreneurial arm of the Society. It shall be composed of the Secretary of Business
Affairs as the Chief Officer, the Assistant Secretary as the second-in-command, and such

(2) The powers and duties of the Office of Business Affairs shall include:


a. Spearhead the development of income-generating projects, including the sale of
Society merchandise, publication of paid journals, and the management of
ticketed events or tournaments;
b. In coordination with the Office of Public Affairs, manage the commercial brand
of the Society to ensure that all business ventures maintain the prestige of the
Pillars;
c. In coordination with the Office of External Affairs, manage the commercial

the Society receives the best value for its investments;
d. Research and implement modern funding models, including digital subscriptions,
crowdfunding, and the commercialization of the Society’s intellectual property;
e. Provide the High Council with long-term economic forecasts and sustainability

funding.
Section 11. Office of Public Affairs

d. Manage, design, and maintain the Official Website of the BSU Debate Society.

**Article 9.
THE COUNCIL OF HOUSE CHANCELLORS**


Section 1. Declaration of the Council of House Chancellors.
The Council of House Chancellors shall be the supreme legislative and representative body of
the Society. It shall be composed of the House Chancellors of the Four Houses who serve as the
primary custodians of their respective House legacies and the voices of their constituents within
the High Council.
Section 2. General Provisions of the Council of House Chancellors
(1) The Council of House Chancellors shall serve as the supreme deliberative, regulatory,
and policy-formulation chamber of the Society, except to the extent reserved to the
people by the Constitutional provision on initiative and referendum. In the pursuit of
Enlightenment and the defense of the Four Pillars, the Council shall exercise the
following unified powers:
(a) To author, deliberate upon, and enact the Blue Book, a comprehensive manual
governing the Society’s operational mechanics. This includes the codification of
parliamentary procedures, the management of the House Point System, and the
establishment of rigorous financial requisition protocols to ensure institutional
transparency;
(b) To establish and maintain the Red Book, translating the virtues of Leadership,
Truth, Scholarship, and the Arts into a binding ethical framework for all members,
and to amend such rules as necessary for the continuous benefit and evolution of
the Society;
(c) To draft the official position of the Society regarding pressing societal issues. A
proposed stance requires the affirmative vote of three out of four House
Chancellors and the concurrence of the President. Upon dual approval, the
Society shall issue a formal statement, the publication and dissemination of which
remains at the discretion of the President; provided that, in exercising such
discretion, the issued statement shall clearly and faithfully represent the specific
position adopted by the Society through the Council of House Chancellors;
(d) To author and deliberate upon formal policy propositions that the Society intends
to lobby to the Benguet State University Administration or the Local Government
Unit (LGU). Such propositions require the final approval of the President before


they are officially introduced to external bodies by the Office of External Affairs,
acting upon the President's direction;
Section 3. Composition of the Council of House Chancellors.
(1) The Council of House Chancellors shall be a four-member deliberative body,
representing the core pillars of the Society. The composition is as follows:
(a) The Presiding Officer. The Vice President of the Society shall serve as the
ex-officio Presiding Officer. The Vice President shall maintain order, facilitate the
agenda, and certify all legislative acts. The Vice President shall have no vote
except in the event of a tie.
(b) The Council consists of one (1) Chancellor from each of the Four Houses, who
serve as the primary representatives of their respective constituents: (i) Chancellor
of House Bathala, (ii) Chancellor of House Laon, (iii) Chancellor of House
Manama, (iv) Chancellor of House Kabunian.
(c) The Council Secretary shall be appointed by the Vice President from among the
various House Secretariat Directors to serve as the primary recording officer and
custodian of the Council’s legislative records.
**Article 10.
THE HIGH TRIBUNAL**
Section 1. Declaration of the High Tribunal
The judicial power of the Society shall be vested in the High Tribunal. It shall serve as the court
of original and final jurisdiction for all cases involving the interpretation of this Constitution,
grievances between Houses, and serious breaches of the Red Book.
Section 2. Composition of the High Tribunal
The High Tribunal shall be composed of the President, who shall serve as the Presiding Judge,
and the four (4) House Chancellors, who shall serve as the Jury of the Pillars. The Executive
Secretary of the High Council shall serve as the Secretary of the Tribunal, responsible for the
intake and technical processing of all petitions. No case shall proceed to trial without a formal
determination of sufficiency by the President, ensuring that the Tribunal’s time is reserved for
matters of significant institutional merit.


Section 3. In the pursuit of institutional justice, the Tribunal shall adopt a specialized adversarial
mandate. The Chancellor of the House to which the respondent belongs is constitutionally bound
to act as the Counsel for the Defense. Conversely, the remaining three Chancellors shall
constitute the Commission on Prosecution, representing the standing of the Society and the
sanctity of its laws.
Section 4. Upon the conclusion of the trial and after due deliberation with the Jury of the Pillars,
the President, as the Presiding Judge, shall deliver the final verdict. The verdict must clearly state
the findings of fact and the specific provisions of the Red Book or Constitution that were
applied.
Section 5. Any party aggrieved by the verdict of the High Tribunal reserves the right to a
singular appeal. In such instances, a Re-Trial shall be convened, presided over by the Society’s
Chief Adviser. The Adviser shall sit as the presiding authority to review the evidence and the
law, and their decision in the appellate trial shall be final, executory, and beyond further
challenge.
**Article 10.
THE COMMISSION ON ELECTION
Article 7.
ELECTIONS AND VOTES**
_Section 1._ Elections must be held at a general meeting. Officers must be nominated and decided
through a majority vote.
_Section 2_. The electoral process may be subject to provisions determined from time to time by
the Executive Committee, consistent with the constitution.
**Article 8.
MEETINGS**


_Section 1_. The society shall have general meetings at least once (1) a month and officers must
have regular meetings once (1) a month.
_Section 2_. Special meetings shall be scheduled by the Executive chairperson as needed.
_Section 3_. The quorum shall be constituted of a majority of the officers and members. Only
present members shall be allocated one (1) vote. All decisions approved during the meeting shall
be final and executory.
**Article 9.
AMENDMENT AND REVISION**
_Section 1_. Any member and officer of the society may request for an amendment and/or a
revision of the constitution and by-law, which will be subject to a deliberation among the
officers.
Section 2. Any amendment or revision of the constitution and By-Laws will be carried with
majority vote in a general meeting.
**Article 10.
DEPENDABILITY AND SEPARABILITY CLAUSE**
_Section 1_. The Debate Society constitution does not repeal the existing main constitution of the
University.
_Section 2_. If any article in this constitution shall be held invalid, the other articles will not be
affected and will remain in full effect.
**Article 11.
EFFECTIVITY CLAUSE**
_Section 1_. The constitution and by-laws will be effective after the ratification of members during
a meeting called for the purpose.
_Section 2_. Publication of the constitution and by-laws shall be made available to all members
upon request.
