Benguet State University

Debate Society

Constitution

Article III

Declaration of Principles

Section 1. The Society shall uphold the transformative power of reasoned dialogue.

It recognizes that civil and intellectual debate is not merely an exercise in rhetoric, but a lifelong discipline essential to the pursuit of truth, critical thought, and personal development.

Section 2. The Society shall promote and protect the principles of diversity and inclusion.

It affirms the importance of integrating the contributions, presence, and perspectives of individuals from various backgrounds into the community, and shall actively advocate for awareness of these values among its members and the broader local community.

Section 3. The Society shall adhere to the principles of democratic leadership, discipline, cooperation, industry, brotherhood, and harmony among its constituents, and shall provide appropriate avenues for the development and application of such competencies.
Section 4. The Society shall recognize and uphold the vital ties among students, faculty, administration, and other sectors of the academic community within Benguet State University.

It shall serve as a platform for the peaceful and intellectual discussion of issues, concerns, and institutional initiatives.

Section 5. The Society and its members shall uphold the highest standards of honesty, fairness, and ethical conduct.

All actions undertaken in the name of the Society shall be guided by a shared sense of responsibility: to truth, to one another, and to the duties entrusted to each member.

Section 6. The Society shall promote an enduring appreciation of Filipino culture, heritage, and values.

It shall advocate for a form of patriotism grounded in critical love, compassion, civic engagement, and social responsibility, and shall reject all expressions of exclusion, hostility, or prejudice.

Section 7. The Society shall cultivate a spirit of constructive and healthy competition among its members and constituent Houses.

All competitions and contests shall be designed to inspire excellence, deepen commitment, and strengthen both individual character and collective bonds.

Section 8. The Society shall preserve its meaningful traditions while remaining open to innovation and reform.

It affirms that institutional relevance requires adaptability, and that traditions must evolve in response to the changing needs of its members and the broader society.

Section 9. The Society shall regard membership not merely as a temporary affiliation, but as a continuous process of growth: intellectually, socially, and morally.

It shall strive to form individuals who are articulate, principled, and prepared to lead both within and beyond the organization.

Section 10. The Society shall affirm that education is not solely the acquisition of knowledge, but also the cultivation of awareness, empathy, and agency.

It shall serve as a platform for empowerment through discourse, recognizing that learning is a means to liberation, personally, socially, and morally.

Section 11. The Society shall uphold the responsible and ethical integration of emerging technologies, including artificial intelligence, into debate preparation, research, and administrative operations.

The Society recognizes that technological advancement must serve the pursuit of truth and intellectual integrity, not circumvent them. All members and officers shall ensure that technological tools are employed transparently, with proper attribution, and in strict compliance with academic standards, thereby safeguarding the authenticity of discourse and honoring the Pillar of Kabunian through responsible stewardship of knowledge. These technological practices shall be interpreted consistently with this Constitution and shall not infringe upon the intellectual property rights or academic freedoms guaranteed to members under Article 4.

Section 12. The Society shall integrate environmental sustainability into its procurement practices, event planning, and daily operations.

Recognizing that intellectual discourse thrives within a healthy and resilient community, the Society shall prioritize eco-conscious resource allocation, minimize waste, and promote sustainable engagement with its physical and digital environments. This commitment to ecological responsibility shall be guided by the Pillar of Laon's emphasis on long-term stewardship and shall be harmonized with the Society's operational mandates without compromising its core mission or the rights of its members under Article 4.

Section 13. The Society shall prioritize the holistic well-being and mental health of its members in the design, scheduling, and execution of all activities and competitive engagements.

Acknowledging that rigorous intellectual pursuit must be balanced with psychological resilience, the Society shall foster a supportive environment that normalizes rest, provides accessible wellness resources, and rejects cultures of burnout or excessive strain. This principle shall be applied in a manner that upholds the Pillar of Manama's dedication to human dignity and shall be construed in full alignment with the fundamental rights and protections afforded to members under Article 4.

Section 14. The Society shall establish and maintain a confidential, non-retaliatory mechanism for the reporting of misconduct, ethical violations, or administrative malfeasance.

Any member who discloses information in good faith regarding breaches of the Constitution, financial irregularities, or threats to institutional integrity shall be protected from retaliation, intimidation, or adverse action. This safeguard shall ensure accountability while preserving the presumption of innocence and due process rights enshrined in Article 4, and shall be interpreted consistently with the Society's commitment to the Pillar of Bathala's foundational values of truth, transparency, and principled leadership.

Section 15. The Society shall cultivate a structured and meaningful relationship with its alumni, recognizing their enduring contributions to the organization's legacy and intellectual tradition.

Formal alumni engagement shall be facilitated through advisory roles, mentorship programs, and institutional partnerships, provided that such involvement remains strictly consultative and does not supersede the autonomous decision-making authority of current student officers, the High Council, or the House Councils. Alumni participation shall be governed by clear operational boundaries that preserve student-led governance while honoring the Pillar of Kabunian's legacy of continuous knowledge transmission, and shall be interpreted in full conformity with the constitutional framework established herein.

⚖️

Constitutional Authority

The Constitution of the BSU Debate Society, known as The Red Book, is the supreme governing document of the organization. All policies, rules, and procedures must conform to its provisions.
