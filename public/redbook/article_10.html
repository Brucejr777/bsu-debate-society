Constitution

Article X

The High Tribunal

Section 1. Declaration of the High Tribunal

The judicial power of the Society shall be vested in the High Tribunal. It shall serve as the court of original and final jurisdiction for all cases involving the interpretation of this Constitution, grievances between Houses, and serious breaches of the Red Book.

Section 2. Composition of the High Tribunal

(1) The High Tribunal shall be composed of the President, who shall serve as the Presiding Judge, and the four (4) House Chancellors, who shall serve as the Jury of the Pillars. The Executive Secretary of the High Council shall serve as the Secretary of the Tribunal, responsible for the intake and technical processing of all petitions. No case shall proceed to trial without a formal determination of sufficiency by the President.

(2) Recusal Protocol for Point Disputes. In proceedings under Article I, Section 2(7) of the Rules and Procedures concerning House Point System disputes, the Chancellor of the House directly involved shall serve as Defense Chancellor per Section 3 and shall recuse themselves from voting. The "Jury of the Pillars" for such matters shall consist of the three uninvolved Chancellors, whose majority vote shall decide the point transaction. This protocol shall not apply to disciplinary or constitutional interpretation cases.

Section 3. In the pursuit of institutional justice, the Tribunal shall adopt a specialized adversarial mandate.

The Chancellor of the House to which the respondent belongs is constitutionally bound to act as the Counsel for the Defense. Conversely, the remaining three Chancellors shall constitute the Commission on Prosecution, representing the standing of the Society and the sanctity of its laws.

Section 4. Upon the conclusion of the trial and after due deliberation with the Jury of the Pillars, the President, as the Presiding Judge, shall deliver the final verdict.

The verdict must clearly state the findings of fact and the specific provisions of the Red Book or Constitution that were applied.

Section 5. Any party aggrieved by the verdict of the High Tribunal reserves the right to a singular appeal.

In such instances, a Re-Trial shall be convened, presided over by the Society's Chief Adviser. The Adviser shall sit as the presiding authority to review the evidence and the law, and their decision in the appellate trial shall be final, executory, and beyond further challenge.

⚖️

Constitutional Authority

The Constitution of the BSU Debate Society, known as The Red Book, is the supreme governing document of the organization. All policies, rules, and procedures must conform to its provisions.
