Constitution

Article VI

The Society Houses

Section 1. Declaration of the Society Houses

The Society shall be composed of four permanent and chartered Houses, each representing a unique philosophical, cultural, and symbolic identity that reflects the values of the Society. These Houses shall serve as the foundational sub-organizations of the Society and shall act as the primary units for competitive debate, truth-seeking, scholarly inquiry, community-building, and leadership development.

Section 2. Establishment.

(1) There shall be four permanent Houses of the Society, established in honor of the four foundational student leaders who chartered the Debate Society. Each House shall also maintain its own motto, emblem, color, and values, subject to approval by the House Society. Houses shall have autonomy over their membership policies, internal structure, traditions, and activities, so long as they remain consistent with the Constitution and Policies of the Society. These Houses shall be known as: House of Bathala, House of Laon, House of Manama, House of Kabunian.

(2) A group of Society members may apply for the formal establishment of a new House by submitting a House Charter to the Council. The Charter must include, but shall not be limited to:

(a) The proposed House motto and core values;

(b) The intended structure of its House Council;

(c) Its initial membership policy;

(d) A code of conduct and internal rules.

(3) All House Charters must be in full accordance with the Constitution and policies of the Society. Any provisions inconsistent with Society principles shall be subject to revision or rejection.

⚖️

Constitutional Authority

The Constitution of the BSU Debate Society, known as The Red Book, is the supreme governing document of the organization. All policies, rules, and procedures must conform to its provisions.
