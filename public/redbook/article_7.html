Article VII

The House Councils

Section 1. Declaration of the House Councils.

(1) Each House shall be governed by a House Council, which shall serve as the highest governing body within the House. It shall have full authority over the internal affairs of the House, subject to the overarching Constitution and policies of the Society.

(2) All official House policies, amendments, and internal rules shall be enacted through formal House Council Resolutions. A resolution shall require the concurrence of a majority of the sitting House Council members and the approval of the House Chancellor to be considered valid. Each resolution shall be properly documented, dated, and entered into the House Chronicle or Records.

Section 2. The functions of the House Council shall be:

(1) Formulate and implement internal policies, plans, and initiatives consistent with the House Charter, serve as the supreme decision-making authority within the House, and uphold and promote the House's values, traditions, and identity;

(2) Manage the recruitment, selection, and induction of new members in accordance with the approved House Membership Policy, monitor member participation, engagement, and standing within the House, recommend or implement probationary systems, mentorship programs, and similar member development efforts;

(3) Organize and coordinate worthwhile student activities designed to improve the general welfare of the students, as well as to prepare them for constructive citizenship and to develop sound and critical thinking among members;

(4) Represent the House in the Council of House Chancellors and in communications with the Society High Council, ensure the House is informed of Society-wide policies, deadlines, and events, and serve as the intermediary in matters requiring coordination between Houses;

(5) Enforce the House's internal code of conduct and values, investigate and address minor violations or conflicts among House members, and recommend disciplinary action, including suspension or expulsion, subject to review by the High Council, through the Internal Affairs Office, for serious offenses;

(6) Manage the House's treasury, prepare and maintain transparent financial records and submit them to the High Council, through the Finance and Resource Management Office, and ensure all expenditures serve the interests and mission of the House;

(7) Maintain the integrity of the House's motto, emblem, color, and cultural identity, document important events, programs, and achievements in an official House chronicle or report, and promote a sense of pride, continuity, and historical awareness among members;

(8) Regularly assess House operations and member satisfaction, submit semester reports on House performance, activities, and membership to the Society Secretariat Director and propose amendments to the House Charter, subject to the approval of the House members, with the concurrence of the President.

(9) Execute specific mandates and duties as may be directed by the President, provided that such instructions are conducive to the general welfare and holistic development of the Houses, the Society, and the community at large.

(10) Mandatory Society Sustainability Levy. Notwithstanding the treasury management authority granted under subsection (6), each House Council shall remit twenty percent (20%) of its unrestricted semester-end surplus funds to the Society General Fund as a mandatory Society Sustainability Levy. Such levy shall be administered by the Office of Financial and Resource Affairs (OFRA) and allocated exclusively for Society-wide operational reserves, emergency contingencies, and inter-House capacity building. The remaining eighty percent (80%) shall remain under the exclusive management of the respective House Council. Funds subject to external donor restrictions, university grants, or legally encumbered obligations shall be exempt from this levy. The High Council shall publish a semi-annual allocation report detailing how levy funds are utilized.

Section 3. The House Council shall be the primary governing body of the House, composed of officers duly elected or appointed to lead its various departments.

The Council shall be organized into Executive Offices, each overseen by a Director. The composition of the House Council shall be determined through a formal House Council Resolution, approved by a majority of the Council and signed by the House Chancellor. While each House may establish additional or modified positions in accordance with its internal needs and traditions, the standard organizational structure shall consist of the following:

(1) The Office of the House Chancellor.

The House Chancellor, who shall be elected by a direct vote from the House members, shall serve as the head of the House Council. The powers and duties of the House Chancellor shall be:

(a) Presides over all House Council meetings and House Assemblies;

(b) Approves House Council Resolutions;

(c) Exercises executive oversight over House programs and governance;

(d) Represents the House in official Society-wide assemblies and external affairs;

(e) May veto any House Council Resolution, subject to override by a two-thirds vote; and

(f) Shall be a member of the Council of House Chancellors.

(g) Performs such other duties as may be assigned by the House Council or the President.

(h) Conflict of Interest Recusal. Any officer of the House Council shall recuse themselves from deliberation, discussion, and voting on any matter in which they have a direct or indirect financial interest, a competitive advantage relative to other Houses or members, or a personal relationship that may reasonably be perceived to impair their impartiality. Specific scenarios requiring recusal shall include:

(i) matters involving procurement, contracting, or financial allocation where the officer or an immediate family member stands to gain materially;

(ii) disciplinary proceedings involving the officer, a close relative, or a member with whom the officer has a significant personal or professional relationship;

(iii) competitive evaluations, point allocations, or award determinations where the officer's House or affiliated members are direct beneficiaries; and

(iv) any matter where the officer has participated in prior unofficial consultations that may compromise objective judgment.

Prior to the commencement of any agenda item, officers shall formally disclose any potential conflict to the House Council, and such disclosure shall be recorded in the official minutes of the meeting. Failure to disclose a conflict of interest, or participation in a matter following a required recusal, shall constitute a Major Violation under Article VI of the Rules and Procedures and may result in sanctions including censure, suspension from voting privileges, or removal from office pursuant to disciplinary procedures. All recusal determinations shall be subject to review by the Society Office of Internal Affairs upon petition by any House member.

(2) The Office of the House Vice Chancellor.

The House Vice Chancellor shall serve as the second-in-rank of the House Council and shall be elected by a direct vote of the House membership. The powers and duties of the House Vice Chancellor shall be:

(a) Assists and substitutes for the Chancellor in their absence;

(b) Oversees coordination between officers and monitors internal execution;

(c) Assists the House Chancellor in the preparation of reports for the Council of House Chancellors;

(d) Performs such other duties as may be assigned by the House Chancellor or the House Council.

(3) The Office of the Secretariat.

The Secretariat Director shall be appointed by the House Chancellor. The Director shall lead the Office of the Secretariat and serve as the third-in-rank officer of the House Council. The powers and duties of the Secretariat Office shall be:

(a) The primary custodian of all House records, responsible for the drafting of official documents, the recording of minutes for all House meetings and assemblies;

(b) Shall maintain a systematic record of all House Council Resolutions and Policy Mandates, ensuring they are accessible to all members for reference;

(c) Shall be responsible for the documentation of the House Chronicle, recording the achievements, major events, and historical milestones of the House during their term; and

(d) Performs such other duties as may be assigned by the House Chancellor or the House Council.

(4) The Office of Internal Affairs.

The Director of Internal Affairs shall be appointed by the respective House Chancellor. The Director shall lead the Office of Internal Affairs and serve as the primary officer responsible for the internal cohesion, discipline, and welfare of the House members. The powers and duties of the Internal Affairs Office shall be:

(a) Enforce the House's internal code of conduct and values, ensuring that all members uphold the prestige and integrity of the Society;

(b) Maintain the official House Membership Database, monitor the participation, engagement, and semesterly standing of each member;

(c) Act as the primary mediator in internal disputes or conflicts among House members, fostering a culture of mutual respect and cooperation;

(d) Monitor the general well-being of members and oversee the implementation of probationary systems, mentorship programs, and similar member-development efforts;

(e) Propose, design, and implement programs and activities aimed at the holistic development of members;

(f) Investigate minor violations of House policies and recommend formal disciplinary actions to the House Council, subject to the review of the Society's Internal Affairs Office;

(g) Manage the recruitment and selection of new members in accordance with the approved House Membership Policy.

(h) Performs such other duties as may be assigned by the House Chancellor or the House Council.

(5) The Office of External Affairs.

The Director of External Affairs shall be appointed by the respective House Chancellor. The director shall lead the Office of External Affairs and serve as the primary liaison between the House and all entities beyond its internal membership. The powers and duties of the Director of External Affairs shall be:

(1) Serve as the primary point of contact for coordination with other Houses, fostering a spirit of friendly rivalry and collaborative growth within the Society;

(2) Assist the House Chancellor in representing the House's interests before the Council of House Chancellors and the Society High Council;

(3) Propose and lead initiatives, programs, and partnerships that extend the House's impact to the community at large, aligning with the Society's mission of constructive citizenship;

(4) Identify and establish partnerships with external organizations, sponsors, or academic institutions that can provide resources, venues, or opportunities for the House;

(5) Lead the planning and execution of joint activities, inter-House competitions, and Society-wide events, ensuring the House's active and prestigious participation;

(6) Performs such other duties as may be assigned by the House Chancellor or the House Council.

(6) The Office of Financial and Resource Affairs.

The Director of Financial and Resource Affairs shall be appointed by the House Chancellor and shall lead the Office of Financial and Resource Affairs. This Office shall serve as the chief fiscal department and the quartermaster of the House. The powers and duties of the Financial and Resource Affairs Office shall be:

(a) Act as the primary custodian of the House Treasury, ensuring the secure collection, management, and documentation of all funds, including membership dues and revenues;

(b) Maintain a comprehensive and updated Inventory of House Assets; oversee the procurement, systematic storage, and maintenance of all physical equipment, supplies, and digital resources;

(c) Develop the semesterly House Budget in coordination with the House Chancellor, ensuring the optimal allocation of resources toward the House's strategic goals;

(d) Manage the distribution and retrieval of House properties for official events, ensuring all materials are functional, accounted for, and returned to storage;

(e) Maintain a transparent and systematic ledger of all transactions; prepare and publish regular financial and resource audits for review by the House Council;

(f) Conduct periodic assessments of the condition of House equipment and propose the acquisition or disposal of assets based on the House's operational needs;

(g) Propose and implement programs focused on financial literacy, resource sustainability, and the optimization of House assets for the benefit of the general membership;

(h) Performs such other duties as may be assigned by the House Chancellor or the House Council.

(7) The Office of Business Affairs.

The Director of Business Affairs shall be appointed by the House Chancellor and shall lead the Office of Business Affairs. This Office shall serve as the primary revenue-generating and entrepreneurial wing of the House. The powers and duties of the Business Affairs Office shall be:

(a) Conceptualize, propose, and spearhead Income-Generating Projects (IGPs) to ensure the financial sustainability and independence of the House;

(b) Manage the production and sale of official House merchandise, ensuring that all products uphold the House's visual identity and prestige;

(c) Identify and negotiate partnerships, sponsorships, or advertisements with external entities and local businesses to provide additional funding or resources for House projects;

(d) Conduct surveys and research within the House and the wider Society to determine the demand for specific products, services, or events;

(e) Prepare comprehensive business plans, project proposals, and post-activity financial statements for every IGP, ensuring all ventures are professional and transparent;

(f) Propose and implement activities that develop the entrepreneurial skills of House members, such as business pitching competitions or financial modeling workshops;

(g) Perform such other duties as may be assigned by the House Chancellor or the House Council.

(8) The Office of Public Affairs.

The Director of Public Affairs shall be appointed by the House Chancellor and shall lead the Office of Public Affairs. This Office shall serve as the official media, branding, and information wing of the House. The powers and duties of the Public Affairs Office shall be:

(a) Serve as the primary manager of all official House social media accounts, ensuring the timely posting of announcements, event updates, and member spotlights;

(b) Lead the conceptualization and creation of all visual materials, including posters, infographics, digital layouts, and promotional videos, ensuring they meet the House's aesthetic standards;

(c) Act as the custodian of the House's brand; ensure that all public-facing materials correctly use the official House logos, colors, and typography;

(d) Act as the public information office for the House, ensuring that all members and the general public are well-informed of House activities, resolutions, and official stances;

(e) Perform such other duties as may be assigned by the House Chancellor or the House Council.

Constitutional Authority

The Constitution of the BSU Debate Society, known as The Red Book, is the supreme governing document of the organization. All policies, rules, and procedures must conform to its provisions.
