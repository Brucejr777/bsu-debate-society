Article IX

The Council of House Chancellors

Section 1. Declaration of the Council of House Chancellors.

The Council of House Chancellors shall be the supreme legislative and representative body of the Society. It shall be composed of the House Chancellors of the Four Houses who serve as the primary custodians of their respective House legacies and the voices of their constituents within the High Council.

(1) The Council of House Chancellors shall serve as the supreme deliberative, regulatory, and policy-formulation chamber of the Society, except to the extent reserved to the people by the Constitutional provision on initiative and referendum. In the pursuit of Enlightenment and the defense of the Four Pillars, the Council shall exercise the following unified powers:

(a) To author, deliberate upon, and enact the Blue Book, a comprehensive manual governing the Society's operational mechanics. This includes the codification of parliamentary procedures, the management of the House Point System, and the establishment of rigorous financial requisition protocols to ensure institutional transparency;

(b) To establish and maintain the Red Book, translating the virtues of Leadership, Truth, Scholarship, and the Arts into a binding ethical framework for all members, and to amend such rules as necessary for the continuous benefit and evolution of the Society;

(c) To draft the official position of the Society regarding pressing societal issues. A proposed stance requires the affirmative vote of three out of four House Chancellors and the concurrence of the President. Upon dual approval, the Society shall issue a formal statement, the publication and dissemination of which remains at the discretion of the President; provided that, in exercising such discretion, the issued statement shall clearly and faithfully represent the specific position adopted by the Society through the Council of House Chancellors;

(d) To author and deliberate upon formal policy propositions that the Society intends to lobby to the Benguet State University Administration or the Local Government Unit (LGU). Such propositions require the final approval of the President before they are officially introduced to external bodies by the Office of External Affairs, acting upon the President's direction;

Section 3. Composition of the Council of House Chancellors.

(1) The Council of House Chancellors shall be a four-member deliberative body, representing the core pillars of the Society. The composition is as follows:

(a) The Presiding Officer. The Vice President of the Society shall serve as the ex-officio Presiding Officer. The Vice President shall maintain order, facilitate the agenda, and certify all legislative acts. The Vice President shall have no vote except in the event of a tie.

(b) The Council consists of one (1) Chancellor from each of the Four Houses, who serve as the primary representatives of their respective constituents: (i) Chancellor of House Bathala, (ii) Chancellor of House Laon, (iii) Chancellor of House Manama, (iv) Chancellor of House Kabunian.

(c) The Council Secretary shall be appointed by the Vice President from among the various House Secretariat Directors to serve as the primary recording officer and custodian of the Council's legislative records.

⚖️

Constitutional Authority

The Constitution of the BSU Debate Society, known as The Red Book, is the supreme governing document of the organization. All policies, rules, and procedures must conform to its provisions.
