Constitution

Article IV

Rights of the Members

Section 1. The right of all the members for freedom to express, freedom of speech, and freedom to peaceful assembly, not contrary to the Constitution and Policies of the Society and existing laws governing such rights, against the Society.
Section 2. The right of all members to file a candidacy or be appointed for the highest governing body of the Society or their respective House's governing body position as prescribed by the provisions of this Constitution.
Section 3. The right to exercise suffrage, ensuring their participation in choosing representatives and voicing opinions on Society's governance matters.
Section 4. The right of all members to petition the proper authorities through the organization for redress of grievances, and present views and opinions conducive to broad, sound, and healthy thinking of vital issues concerning the welfare of the students.
Section 5. The right of all members to information on matters of public concern shall be recognized.

Access to official records, documents, and papers pertaining to official acts, transactions, or decisions, as well as to research data used as basis for policy development, shall be afforded by all members, subject to such limitations as may be provided by the Constitution and Policies of the Society.

Section 6. The right of every member to be informed of the rules and regulations governing him.
Section 7. No member shall be subjected to exploitation, involuntary servitude or cruel or unusual punishment by the highest governing body of the Society or their respective House's highest governing body.
Section 8. The right of all members to form, assist, and join school-based and non-school-based organizations, alliances, and federations for their integral development.
Section 9. The rights of all members to avail all the facilities and resources of the organization for school related activities within the school campus, subject to limitations as may be provided by their respective House's highest governing body or the highest governing body of the Society.
Section 10. No member shall be subject to punishments, sanctions, or fines, solely by reason of his political beliefs and aspirations.
Section 11. In all punishment prosecutions conducted by the High Council or High Tribunal, the accused shall be presumed innocent until the contrary is proved, and shall enjoy the right to be heard by himself and counsel, to be informed of the nature and cause of the accusation against him, to have a speedy, impartial, and public trial, to meet the witnesses face to face, and to have compulsory process to secure the attendance of witnesses and the production of evidence in his behalf.

For clarity, the public trial and compulsory process requirements of this Section shall not apply to administrative hearings conducted by House Councils for Minor Violations under the Rules and Procedures, provided such hearings guarantee the Respondent's fundamental rights to written notice, to be heard, to present evidence, and to receive a written decision. However, after arraignment, the trial may proceed notwithstanding the absence of the accused, provided that he has been duly notified and his failure to appear is unjustifiable.

Section 12. No member shall be compelled to be a witness against himself.

Constitutional Authority

The Constitution of the BSU Debate Society, known as The Red Book, is the supreme governing document of the organization. All policies, rules, and procedures must conform to its provisions.
